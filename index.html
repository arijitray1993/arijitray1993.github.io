As AI expands the scope of human creativity, I wish to solve the gaps in machine intelligence to enable the next generation of human-machine collaborative tasks.

About me:

I am a second-year Computer Vision Ph.D. Student at Boston University (BU), advised by Profs. Kate Saenko and Bryan Plummer. I collaborate closely with Prof. Ranjay Krishna from the University of Washington. I recieved my M.S from Virginia Tech, advised by Prof. Devi Parikh.

Recently, I am interested in adapting multimodal language models to reason compositionally about the visual and physical world. I am driven by community and using AI to enable societal good. Hence, I am a member of the student leadership committee for the AI for Impact Venture Studio at MIT. I am also a part of the BU + UC Berkeley Team in the DARPA Semantic Forensics program for detecting misinformation and radicalizing content.

I am always interested to collaborate with motivated students. If you are interested to work on multimodal models to help human creativity, say hi!
